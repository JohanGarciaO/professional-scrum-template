# Product Vision

Define the long-term purpose and direction of your product.

---

## About this document

This document serves both as a learning guide and as a project template.

If this is your first time creating a Product Vision, read the explanatory sections before filling out the template.

Once you understand how to use this document, feel free to remove the explanatory sections and keep only the completed template for your project.

---

## Purpose

A Product Vision describes **why your product exists**.

It defines the value your product aims to deliver, the audience it serves, and the long-term direction that guides future decisions.

A well-defined Product Vision helps the team make consistent decisions throughout the project's lifecycle.

It should remain relatively stable, even as requirements and features evolve.

---

## Who is responsible?

The Product Vision is typically created and maintained by the **Product Owner**.

However, it should be shared with the entire team to ensure everyone understands the purpose of the product.

---

## When should it be created?

The Product Vision should be defined before creating the Product Backlog.

It provides the foundation for every feature, user story, and Sprint planned during development.

Whenever there is uncertainty about priorities, the Product Vision should be used as a decision-making guide.

---

## How to write a good Product Vision

A good Product Vision answers the following questions:

- What problem does the product solve?
- Who are the target users?
- What value does the product provide?
- What makes this product worth building?

Focus on the problem and the value delivered rather than implementation details or specific features.

A Product Vision should remain concise, easy to understand, and inspiring for the team.

## Template

### Product Name

> Replace this text with your product name.

### Vision Statement

> Describe the long-term purpose of your product.

### Target Audience

> Describe the main problem your product solves.

> Explain the value your product delivers.

### Success Vision

> Describe what success looks like if your product achieves its purpose.

---

## Example

### Product Name

SkyBoard

### Vision Statement

Enable travel agencies to manage airline reservations through a centralized, reliable, and intuitive platform.

### Target Audience

Small and medium-sized travel agencies.

### Problem Statement

Travel agencies often manage reservations using multiple airline systems, resulting in fragmented information, manual work, and operational inefficiencies.

### Value Proposition

Provide a single platform that centralizes reservation management, reduces manual effort, and improves operational efficiency.

### Success Vision

Become the preferred reservation management platform for travel agencies by simplifying daily operations and increasing productivity.

---

## Common Mistakes

### ❌ Describing features instead of purpose

Bad:

> The system will have login, reports, dashboards, and PDF generation.

Good:

> Help travel agencies efficiently manage airline reservations.

---

### ❌ Being too generic

Bad:

> Build the best software.

Good:

> Simplify reservation management for small and medium-sized travel agencies.

---

### ❌ Focusing on technology

Bad:

> Build a Next.js application using PostgreSQL.

Good:

> Create a reliable platform that improves reservation management.
