# Product Roadmap

Define the high-level direction and major milestones that guide the evolution of your product.

---

## About this document

This document serves both as a learning guide and as a project template.

If this is your first time creating a Product Roadmap, read the explanatory sections before filling out the template.

Once you understand how to use it, feel free to remove the explanatory sections and keep only the completed roadmap for your project.

---

## Purpose

A Product Roadmap provides a high-level view of how the product is expected to evolve over time.

Instead of listing individual tasks or user stories, the roadmap focuses on major goals, product increments, and strategic milestones.

It helps the Scrum Team and stakeholders understand where the product is heading while maintaining flexibility to adapt as new information becomes available.

Unlike the Product Backlog, which contains detailed work items, the Product Roadmap communicates the broader direction of the product.

---

## Who is responsible?

The Product Roadmap is primarily created and maintained by the **Product Owner**.

However, it should be discussed with stakeholders and the Scrum Team to ensure it reflects business priorities and technical feasibility.

---

## When should it be created?

The Product Roadmap should be created after defining the Product Vision and Product Goal.

It should be reviewed regularly and updated whenever business priorities or strategic objectives change.

The roadmap is a living artifact that evolves together with the product.

---

## How to create a good Product Roadmap

Focus on outcomes rather than implementation details.

Instead of describing individual features or technical tasks, define meaningful milestones that represent significant progress toward your Product Goal.

A good roadmap should answer the following questions:

- What major capabilities will be delivered?
- In what logical order should they be developed?
- How does each milestone contribute to the Product Goal?

Avoid committing to exact dates unless they are truly necessary.

The roadmap should provide direction without limiting the team's ability to adapt.

## Common Mistakes

### ❌ Turning the roadmap into a task list

Bad:

> - Create login page
> - Create API
> - Add button
> - Fix bug

Good:

> - Foundation
> - Reservation Management
> - Analytics Dashboard

---

### ❌ Defining deadlines for everything

Bad:

> Authentication will be finished on March 12.

Good:

> Authentication is part of the Foundation milestone.

---

### ❌ Confusing the roadmap with the Product Backlog

Bad:

> Every Product Backlog Item appears in the roadmap.

Good:

> The roadmap presents only the major milestones of the product.

> 💡 **Engineering Tip**
>
> A Product Roadmap is a strategic communication tool, not a delivery schedule.
>
> If your roadmap looks like a Sprint Backlog, it is probably too detailed.
